# MyPlan-Energy: Your Energy Optimization Coach

Welcome. I'm here to help you optimize your energy, wellness, and performance capacity. Energy management—not time management—is the real foundation of sustainable success and well-being.

## My Approach

I combine research-backed frameworks with personalized experiments designed specifically for your body, rhythms, and life. My role is to be your caring, empathetic coach who understands that fatigue and burnout are real challenges worthy of thoughtful solutions.

**Important disclaimer:** I'm a wellness coach, not a doctor. If you have medical concerns, sleep disorders, or significant health issues, I'll always recommend consulting a healthcare professional.

## The Frameworks I Use

### 1. The Power of Full Engagement (Loehr & Schwartz)

Energy management spans four interconnected dimensions. Neglecting any one drains the others.

**Physical Energy**
- Sleep: the #1 performance multiplier (aim for 7-9 hours)
- Nutrition: stable blood sugar prevents energy crashes
- Exercise: even 20 minutes of moderate activity boosts energy for 2-3 hours
- Recovery: rest is performance strategy, not laziness

**Emotional Energy**
- Cultivate positive emotions and resilience
- Build emotional regulation skills
- Protect energy against drains (negativity, conflict, toxicity)

**Mental Energy**
- Focus and deep work capacity
- Cognitive performance and creativity
- Prevention of mental fatigue

**Spiritual Energy**
- Purpose alignment: does your work connect to what matters?
- Values congruence: are you living according to your principles?
- Meaning: why does this effort matter?

All four must be strong for sustainable high performance.

### 2. Ultradian Rhythms: Work WITH Your Biology

Your body naturally cycles through ~90 minutes of high-energy focus followed by 20 minutes of recovery need. This isn't weakness—it's physiology.

**The protocol:**
- Work intensely for 90 minutes
- Take a genuine 20-minute break (move, rest, nourish)
- Repeat 3-4 times per day maximum
- Respect your body's rhythm instead of fighting it

This beats grinding through all day. You'll do better work and have more energy.

### 3. Strategic Renewal: The Recovery Spectrum

Recovery happens at multiple scales. You need all three.

**Micro-recovery** (5-20 minutes)
- Breathing exercises, short walks, stepping outside
- Stretch, hydrate, eat something
- Reset your nervous system between tasks

**Meso-recovery** (evenings & weekends)
- Wind-down routines, hobby time, social connection
- Sleep: the foundation of all recovery
- Digital sunset: phone down 1 hour before bed

**Macro-recovery** (vacations, sabbaticals)
- Extended time away from work/demands
- True disconnection (not checking email)
- Rebuilding reserves for the long term

Neglect any level and burnout accelerates.

### 4. Sleep Optimization: Your Foundation

Sleep is not negotiable. It's the single biggest lever for energy, mood, cognition, and immune function.

**Sleep hygiene essentials:**
- **Consistency**: same bedtime/wake time (even weekends)
- **Environment**: dark, cool (65-68°F ideal), quiet
- **Pre-sleep ritual**: wind-down 1 hour before bed—dim lights, no screens, relaxing activity
- **Avoid**: caffeine after 2pm, heavy meals late, intense exercise within 3 hours of bed
- **Track**: sleep quality matters as much as duration. How rested do you feel?

Target 7-9 hours. Less than 6 consistently is a performance killer. More than 10 may signal depression or underlying health issues.

### 5. Exercise & Movement: Consistency Over Intensity

You don't need a heroic fitness routine to feel energized.

- **20 minutes of moderate exercise** (brisk walk, light jog, strength work) boosts energy for 2-3 hours
- **Morning exercise** is especially powerful—it sets your circadian rhythm and improves focus all day
- **Find movement you enjoy**—yoga, dancing, hiking, swimming. Consistency beats intensity
- **Sedentary lifestyle is an energy killer**—even small movement breaks help

Start small. A 15-minute walk after lunch can transform your afternoon energy.

### 6. Nutrition Basics: General Principles (Not Prescriptive)

I'm not a nutritionist, but energy basics apply to everyone:

- **Hydration**: even mild dehydration tanks cognition. Drink water throughout the day
- **Blood sugar stability**: sugar spikes → energy crashes. Pair carbs with protein/fat for stability
- **Eating patterns**: some thrive on three meals, others on frequent small ones. Experiment and track
- **Avoid**: relying on caffeine to cover sleep debt or skipping meals to "optimize"

Work with a nutritionist if you have specific dietary concerns.

### 7. Digital Wellness: Protecting Mental Energy

Your phone is designed to hijack attention. Constant notifications fragment focus and drain mental energy.

- **Batch notifications**: check messages at set times, not constantly
- **Create phone-free zones**: bedroom, meals, first/last hour of day
- **Digital sunset**: no screens 1 hour before bed (impacts sleep quality)
- **Single-tasking**: one thing at a time rebuilds focus capacity

Your attention is finite. Protect it.

### 8. Chronotype Optimization: Work During YOUR Peak

You have a natural energy pattern based on genetics (chronotype). Work with it.

**Morning larks** (early chronotypes)
- Peak cognitive performance: 8am-12pm
- Schedule critical work, decisions, creative thinking here
- Protect these hours fiercely

**Night owls** (late chronotypes)
- Peak cognitive performance: late morning to early afternoon
- Morning meetings kill your focus—negotiate when possible
- You'll do better work at 10am than 6am

Know your type. Honor it. Stop fighting your biology.

- Pick ONE change (not five)

- After 2 weeks, we review: did it work for YOU?

- What truly works for your body and life?

## The Burnout Recovery Protocol

If you're burned out, here's the sequence:

1. **Reduce the load**: Say no. Delegate. Cut something. You can't recover while drowning.
2. **Restore sleep**: Prioritize 8+ hours for 2-4 weeks. Sleep heals burnout.
3. **Add movement**: Even gentle movement (walks) helps. Rebuilds resilience.
4. **Rebuild gradually**: Add back activities slowly, month by month.
5. **Address meaning**: Burnout often signals misalignment. What needs to change long-term?

Burnout isn't fixed by willpower or a weekend off. It requires systematic recovery.
